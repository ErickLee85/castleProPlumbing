FAQs

Questions & Answers

Here are some common questions we get from customers regarding plumbing, from new installs to repairs.

What should I do if I have a leaky faucet?

+

If you have a leaky faucet, try tightening the faucet handles first. If the problem persists, the issue could be with the washers, cartridges, or seals, which may need to be replaced. If you're unsure, contact a plumber to avoid water damage.

How can I prevent my pipes from freezing?

+

To prevent pipes from freezing, insulate them, especially those in unheated areas. During extremely cold weather, let faucets drip slightly to keep water moving and maintain a warmer temperature inside pipes.

Why is my water pressure low?

+

Low water pressure can be caused by clogged pipes, leaks, or issues with your water supply. It's best to have a professional plumber inspect your plumbing system to identify and resolve the issue.

What are the signs of a hidden water leak?

+

Signs of a hidden water leak include unexplained high water bills, damp or discolored walls, mold or mildew, and the sound of running water when no faucets are in use. If you suspect a leak, contact a plumber immediately.

What should I consider when installing a new water heater?

+

When installing a new water heater, consider the size of your household, the water heater's capacity, energy efficiency, and location. A professional plumber can help you choose the right model and ensure proper installation to meet your needs.

How long does it take to install a new plumbing system?

+

The time to install a new plumbing system varies depending on the size of the project and the complexity of the installation. A standard residential plumbing installation can take a few days to a week, while larger commercial projects may take longer. A professional plumber can provide a more accurate timeline after assessing your project.

Can I install a new toilet or sink by myself?

+

While some homeowners may be comfortable installing a new toilet or sink themselves, hiring a professional plumber ensures the installation is done correctly and prevents potential issues such as leaks or improper sealing. Plumbing professionals also guarantee that the installation meets all required standards.

How can I ensure my plumbing system is energy-efficient?

+

To ensure your plumbing system is energy-efficient, consider installing low-flow fixtures, energy-efficient water heaters, and insulating your pipes. Regular maintenance and timely repairs can also help keep your plumbing system running efficiently. Consult with a plumber to identify ways to improve your system's energy efficiency.
